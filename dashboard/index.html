üí° Key Insights

Top 5 Risk Factors for Readmission

1. Number of Inpatient Visits: Patients with prior inpatient visits have significantly higher readmission risk.

2. Discharge Disposition: Where patients go after discharge strongly predicts readmission likelihood.

3. Total Healthcare Visits: Higher overall healthcare utilization indicates complex medical needs.

4. Number of Lab Procedures: More lab tests suggest more severe or complex conditions.

5. Number of Medications: Polypharmacy increases risk of medication errors and non-adherence.

Patient Demographics

The majority of patients are aged 70-80 years (modal age group), with females representing a slightly higher proportion. Caucasian patients make up the largest racial group in the dataset. The average hospital stay is 4.4 days, with significant variation based on diagnosis complexity.

Model Performance

Gradient Boosting achieved the best performance with a ROC AUC score of 0.680, indicating moderate predictive power. While accuracy is high (88.9%), the low recall (1.01%) suggests the model is conservative in predicting readmissions. This trade-off prioritizes precision to avoid unnecessary interventions.

üéØ Business Recommendations

1. Implement Risk Stratification System

Deploy the predictive model to automatically flag high-risk patients before discharge. Integrate the risk score into the electronic health record (EHR) system to alert care teams.

2. Enhanced Discharge Planning

For patients with prior inpatient visits or complex medication regimens, assign dedicated care coordinators to ensure smooth transitions. Provide detailed medication reconciliation and patient education.

3. Post-Discharge Follow-Up Protocol

Establish mandatory 48-hour follow-up calls for high-risk patients. Schedule clinic appointments within 7 days of discharge. Use telehealth for convenient check-ins.

4. Medication Management Program

Create a specialized pharmacy consultation service for patients on 5+ medications. Provide pill organizers and medication calendars. Implement automated refill reminders.

5. Financial Impact

Reducing readmissions by 20% through these interventions could save approximately $5.2 million annually, based on an average readmission cost of $15,000 per patient. This also improves Medicare reimbursement rates by avoiding penalties.
